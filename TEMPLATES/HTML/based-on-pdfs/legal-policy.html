For decades, people across the country have faced criminalization and loss of liberty due to pregnancy status. This document outlines the scope, impact, and systemic drivers of these practices.

Introduction

Across the United States, individuals have been subjected to investigation, arrest, and prosecution based solely on their pregnancy outcomes or perceived risks during pregnancy.

These cases often rely on expansive interpretations of laws that were never intended to apply in this context, resulting in profound consequences for individuals, families, and communities.

Structural Impact

The impact of these practices is not evenly distributed. Low-income individuals, people of color, and those with limited access to healthcare are disproportionately affected.

Fear of surveillance and punishment discourages individuals from seeking medical care, ultimately undermining both maternal and infant health outcomes.
