MLA Citation Style Guide (9th Edition)

Common Works Cited and in-text citation conventions

Source Type Works Cited Format In-Text Citation Key MLA Conventions
Book Last Name, First Name. Title of Book. Publisher, Year. (LastName Page) Italicize standalone works like books.
MLA usually uses author-page format in text.
End the entry with a period.
Journal Article Last Name, First Name. "Title of Article." Journal Title, vol. #, no. #, Year, pp. xx-xx. (LastName Page) Put article titles in quotation marks.
Italicize the journal title.
Include volume, issue, year, and page range when available.
Webpage Last Name, First Name. "Title of Webpage." Website Name, Publisher if different from website name, Day Month Year, URL. (LastName) Use quotation marks for the page title.
Italicize the website name.
Access date is optional, but often recommended when no publication date is given or content is likely to change.
Chapter in an Edited Book Last Name, First Name. "Title of Chapter." Title of Book, edited by First Name Last Name, Publisher, Year, pp. xx-xx. (LastName Page) Chapter title goes in quotation marks.
Book title is italicized.
Include the editor and page range.
Two Authors Last Name, First Name, and First Name Last Name. Title. Publisher, Year. (LastName and LastName Page) In prose, use and, not &.
Include both surnames in the in-text citation.
Three or More Authors Last Name, First Name, et al. Title. Publisher, Year. (LastName et al. Page) Use et al. in both the Works Cited entry and the in-text citation for 3+ authors.
Direct Quote Use the normal Works Cited entry for the source. (LastName 23) MLA typically uses the author’s surname and page number only.
Do not use a comma between name and page number.
No Author Title of Work. Publisher, Year. (Short Title Page) Move the title to the front when no author is listed.
Shorten long titles in in-text citations.
No Date Last Name, First Name. "Title of Work." Website Name, URL. (LastName) MLA does not use n.d. the way APA does in typical MLA examples.
Include whatever publication details are available.
Multiple Sources in One Citation Each source appears separately in the Works Cited list. (LastName 23; OtherName 41) Separate sources in one parenthetical citation with semicolons.
General MLA Template Author. "Title of Source." Title of Container, Other contributors, Version, Number, Publisher, Publication date, Location. Varies by source MLA 9 uses a flexible template built from core elements rather than a single rigid format for every source.
